Attending Weaving Connections

Weaving Connections is happening this week! Please take a moment to read this page — you'll learn everything you need to know to have a safe and enjoyable time with us.

Arriving

Address

Parking

There will be parking in the main lot in front of the greenhouses, but not enough for all of us. If you are able-bodied, please park on the road east of the greenhouse gates.

Here is the greenhouse map which shows parking areas — and be prepared for mud!

Upon Arrival

Enter through the main doors of the greenhouse. The first room is called the "Header House". Restrooms are to the right, and the kitchen is straight ahead.

The Header House is where we'll all meet up initially. There will be a table with name tags, and maps of the greenhouses & workshop locations. The Header House is also where we'll come together to enjoy our meals.

The Greenhouse

We will be weaving in a working, operational greenhouse that is just entering its busiest time of the year. The staff have done their best to make plenty of room for all the willow and weavers this weekend. However, some greenhouses still contain plants, and staff may be working around us on Friday. Feel free to look around, but please be respectful of the work being done to keep the greenhouse running.

Animals

There are three dogs and two cats on the property, all of whom are very friendly. The small black & white border collie is old, deaf, and blind, so she can be a bit nervous around new people. Please be gentle with her and avoid reaching out too quickly, as she may not see you and could get scared.

What to Wear

The temperature in the greenhouse reflects the weather outside. It can be sunny and warm, or quite chilly. Bring appropriate clothing:

  • Warm: short sleeve shirt • summer shoes or sandals • sunscreen • hat
  • Cold: a warm sweater • winter jacket • winter or mud boots

Meals

If you have purchased meals, please bring your own plate, bowl, mug and utensils. We want to create no waste, like a hike: take out what you bring, leave no trace.

If you are bringing a bag lunch, please bring ice. We do have access to a small fridge, but space is limited, and we cannot guarantee there will be room.

The microwave is on the same breaker as our caterer's hot plates. We ask that you wait until 1:15 or 6:15 to heat your meals unless Elvira gives us the go-ahead to use the microwave earlier.

There will be a community snack station! We encourage you to bring snacks, tea, coffee, and anything else that'd make you happy to share with the community — except alcohol. This is a dry event.

There is a small kitchen available for everyone to use, equipped with a sink, microwave, and kettle. The tap water is safe to drink (it's city water).

Activities

Open Weave

Bring your own project to work on, or join in on weaving our forest friend. If you have never woven a willow sculpture before, here are some YouTube videos to get your creative juices flowing: Willow Sculpture Timelapse • Weaving Willow Sheep

We will have a limited number of chairs available in the open weave area. If you have one you'd like to use for yourself, or one you wouldn't mind sharing, bring a chair.

Finally, if you have any to spare, bring an armful of willow to share. We need all the willow we can get.

Sharing your Weaving Adventures

Have you been busy weaving? We have many new weavers joining us this year! Bring some of your work to show off and inspire your fellow weavers.

If you'd like to exchange one of your pieces, place it on the table at the Give and Receive Station — then choose another to take home in return.

Tools for Sale

At designated times throughout the weekend, we will have a small selection of basketry tools and books for sale. You can make payment through CC, cash, or e-transfer. If you have basketry-related books, tools, or materials you would like to sell, you are welcome to sell them in the main greenhouse at this time.

Friday Night — Our First Alberta Basketry Guild AGM!

Join us for our very first Alberta Basketry Guild Annual General Meeting (AGM)! This is our chance to select the team that will lead the way in promoting and growing our basketry community in Alberta. It's also an opportunity for us to come together, share ideas, and shape the future of our guild.

What will our community look like in the coming years? What kinds of activities will we pursue? If you have thoughts, ideas, or special skills to offer, this is your chance to contribute and have your voice heard!

Not a member yet? The best time to register is before the AGM — so register now!

After you register, please take 15 minutes to fill out the Members' Survey.

Non-members are also welcome to attend the AGM.

We look forward to seeing you there!

    Meeting Agenda
  • A short presentation of the annual and financial report
  • Voting on board members and a few housekeeping items
  • Dreaming of Our Future — During this time, we'll have the opportunity to move around, engage in discussions, and share our hopes and dreams for the guild's future. Through artistic and written forms, we'll capture our collective vision.
  • Presentation on Basketry Tools by Cathie Harper
